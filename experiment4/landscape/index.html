Experiment 4 - Generateive Worlds:Landscape

Aritst Statement

This project explores the idea of Infinity by creating a world that extends infinitely in all directions. The infinite terrain is represented by a grid of tiles that can be navigated endlessly, providing a seamless and boundless experience. Each tile dynamically generates features like grass, water, and sand based on its position, reinforcing the concept of an ever-expanding world. Variation in Space is achieved through the distinct appearance of tiles, which change based on their location in the grid. Grass, water, and sand regions transition naturally, with additional details like trees, fish, or cacti appearing in appropriate areas. This variation makes each region feel unique and visually interesting, whether the camera is stationary or moving. Variation in Key allows users to generate entirely new worlds by entering different keys. Each key determines the seed for the random and noise functions, controlling the placement and type of features like terrain, trees, and water. By re-entering a previous key, users can restore a specific world, highlighting how the key influences the generator's structure and appearance. Signs of Life are introduced through the interaction between the audience and the world. Clicking on tiles with grass causes flowers to grow dynamically, with their size, color, and placement determined by the seed and user input. These flowers persist and grow over time, adding an interactive layer that makes the world feel alive and responsive to user actions.
